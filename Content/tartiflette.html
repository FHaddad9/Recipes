Tartiflette

Description

Very popular French dish consisting of potatoes, onions, and bacon casserole with a nice topping of French Reblochon Cheese. This dish has first been eaten during the Roman Empire. It has become popular because of its simple ingredients and warmness to winter time. Sometimes added with crème fraîche or white wine, this delicious creamy dish is very tasty and with the addition of Reblochon cheese, it makes for a stunning dish.

Ingredients

  • 1kg of potatoes
  • 200g smoked bacon
  • 500g of Reblochon Cheese
  • 200ml of white wine
  • 150ml of milk
  • 2 onions
  • 3 eggs
  • 1 tablespoon of butter

Extras

  • 100g of crème fraîche

Steps

  1. Firstly, preheat your oven at 180°C. Melt your unsalted butter and chocolate into a bowl by traditionally melting or microwaving. Mix it until the melted chocolate is nice and shiny.

  2. Get a mixing bowl and add your cracked eggs. Along with the eggs, put in your caster sugar and optionally, your choice of nuts. Whisk until the eggs have mixed well with the sugar and the texture is bubbly. When finished, add the egg mixture onto your melted chocolate.

  3. Mix the chocolate and eggs until the eggs are firmly mixed with the chocolate. Sieve flour onto the mixture and mix until no flour is seen and the brownie mixture is ready for baking.

  4. Pour your mixture onto a buttered and floured pan or onto a pan fitted with baking parchment. Smooth it out so the chocolate covers the whole pan. Optionally add in your chocolate buttons on top.

  5. The pan should now be in the oven at 180°C and should be ready in 18 minutes. To check the readiness, poke a knife into the brownies and it should come out clean. Cut it into however many servings you wish and enjoy!
